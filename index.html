About the project

A new way to visualize the MoMA art collection

I have always struggled to connect art movements throughout the 19th and 20th centuries to their specific time periods. Additionally, I have always wondered which artists overlapped where, where in time did this occur and how might've they influced each other? While I can't answer the latter with data visualization what I can do is develop a system where we can begin to visually understand where these overlaps occur, and ultimately present a different approach to learning about art movements through the 19th and 20th centuries.

Visualization One

A 3D Visualization of Artists, Movements and Time

A 3D representation of the relationship between artists, the movement in which their work was constructed, and the time period in which it was published would allow for a more comprehensive understanding of the artistic landscape. By representing these three factors in three dimensions, we could visualize the interconnections between them, seeing how artists and movements related to each other across time. This visualization could help us better understand the evolution of artistic movements over time, revealing how artists and movements influenced each other and how they responded to changes in the cultural and historical context. Additionally, a 3D representation could reveal patterns and clusters in the data that might not be immediately apparent in a 2D representation, helping us to identify new connections and relationships between artists and movements.

Visualization Two

A 2D Visualization of completed work by artists

This chart defines just how many works the artist completed over their lifetime, allowing us to understand how different the movements these artists contributed to varied in the way art was produced. For example, in the case of Andy Warhol, who was regarded as the 'Pope of Pop' (the pop art movement), used alot of print based process, while Van Gogh depended more on Oil on Canvas for his process.
